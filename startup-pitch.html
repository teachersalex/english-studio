Prologue: The Redline

Interlagos R&D Track â€¢ Dusk

The air smelled of burnt rubber and ozone. On the track, a matte-black shape blurred past at 280 km/h. It was silent, except for the high-pitched whine of the electric motor.

This was the Volt X-Type. Not just a motorcycle. A revolution on two wheels.

In the control tower, Sofia, the Project Lead, watched the telemetry data on her screens. The numbers were green. Good.

"Battery temperature stable," said Marcus, the Lead Engineer. His eyes never left the monitor. "Power output at 95%."

On the track, the test rider, Leo, pushed the bike harder into the final corner.

Suddenly, a red warning light flashed on Sofia's screen. CRITICAL ERROR. SYSTEM OVERHEAT.

"Abort!" Marcus shouted into the radio. "Leo, abort run! Cut power now!"

On the track, the bike wobbled violently. Smoke puffed from the side. Leo wrestled for control, fighting the physics, before stopping just meters from the wall.

Silence returned to the track.

Sofia stared at the red warning. The biggest demo of their lives, for a group of international investors, was in 48 hours.

And their prototype just failed.

Chapter 1: The Post-Mortem

The Volt X-Type sat on a lift in the center of the brightly lit hangar. The panels were removed, revealing the beast's electric heart. The smell of burnt electronics hung in the air.

Chapter 5: The All-Nighter

The Hangar â€¢ 3:00 AM

The hangar was silent. The floor was covered with empty pizza boxes and energy drink cans. The air was heavy with exhaustion.

They were pulling an all-nighter.

The new part was in. The frame was modified. Now came the hardest part: the code.

Marcus sat at a computer, his eyes red. He typed, deleted, typed again.

"It is not syncing," he said, his voice frustrated. "The central computer does not recognize the new German part. It still thinks the old unit is there."

Sofia walked over and put a hand on his shoulder. "Take a break, Marcus. Walk away for five minutes."

"And if you make a mistake because you are tired, it won't matter anyway," she said firmly. "Go. Five minutes. That is an order."

Marcus glared at her, then slumped. He stood up and walked out into the cool night air.

Sofia sat in his chair. She wasn't a coder, but she understood logic. She looked at the screen, at the sea of data. Somewhere in there was the key to their future.

Chapter 6: The Breakthrough

Elena woke up on the sofa. Leo jumped down from the workbench. They all looked at the bike. Outside the window, the sun was rising. The sky was purple and gold.
