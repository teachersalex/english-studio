Prologue: The Stormy Night

Setting: The Rose Kingdom (Another Dimension) - 20 Years Ago
Mood: Dark, Urgent, Sad

The sky was purple and the wind was howling. The Rose Castle was under attack.

Queen Amalia held her baby daughter tight. The baby was sleeping. She had a small birthmark on her wrist shaped like a crown.

"We cannot keep her here," the Queen cried. "The Dark Army is coming. General Vanda wants the throne."

The King looked at the magic portal. It showed a different world. A world with green forests and blue oceans. It showed a country called Brazil.

"I will send her to a safe place," the King said. "I will hide the three Magic Artifacts in this new world. When she is ready, the Compass will wake up. It will guide her home."

The Queen kissed the baby's forehead. "Be safe, my little Marina. You are a princess, and one day, you will return."

They put the baby in a basket and pushed it through the portal. Then, the doors of the castle burst open. General Vanda entered with a sword, but she was too late. The Princess was gone.

Vocabulary

  • To Howl (Verb): To make a long, loud, sad sound (like a wolf or the wind).
  • Tight (Adverb/Adj): Held closely and firmly; not loose.
  • Throne (Noun): The special chair where a King or Queen sits.
  • Safe (Adjective): Not in danger.
  • Forehead (Noun): The part of the face above the eyes and below the hair.
  • Basket (Noun): A container made of straw or cane, used for carrying things.
  • Sword (Noun): A weapon with a long metal blade.

Marina lived in a beautiful old farmhouse in the countryside of S√£o Paulo. It was a quiet place, far from the noise of the big city. Marina loved her simple life. Her bedroom was painted soft pink, and she spent her days baking cakes and reading romance novels. However, she always felt that she was different. She had a small birthmark on her wrist shaped like a crown.

One rainy afternoon, her Aunt Vanda asked her to clean the attic. The attic was at the top of the house. It was dark, cold, and full of dust. Marina found old chairs, broken lamps, and many heavy boxes.

In the corner of the room, behind an old painting, she saw something strange. It was a small wooden chest with silver decorations. Marina opened it carefully. Inside, there was a compass. But it was not a normal compass. It was made of pink rose quartz.

Marina held the compass in her hand. Suddenly, it started to vibrate. A soft pink light filled the dark attic. The needle of the compass did not point North. It pointed somewhere far away.

"Marina!" Aunt Vanda called from downstairs. "What are you doing up there?"

Marina quickly hid the compass in her pocket. "Nothing, Auntie! I found an old toy," she lied. Her heart was beating very fast. She knew her life was about to change.

  • Chest (Noun): A strong box used for keeping valuable things safe.
  • Compass (Noun): An instrument that shows direction (North, South, East, West).
  • To Hide (Verb): To put something in a place where people cannot see it.

Chapter 2: The Historian

Setting: The Town Library
Mood: Romantic, Awkward, Intriguing

The next day, Marina took the bus to the small town nearby. She wanted to find answers about the strange compass. She went to the library. It was a quiet building with tall shelves full of books.

Marina was looking for books about "Magic Crystals" when she dropped her bag. The pink compass fell on the floor with a loud noise. Clack!

A young man picked it up. He was tall, handsome, and wore glasses. He looked very intelligent.

"Careful," he said. He looked at the compass and his eyes opened wide. "Where did you get this? This is the Rose Quartz of the Lost Kingdom."

Marina was shocked. "Who are you?"

"I am Leo," he smiled. "I am a history student from the university. I study legends about magic in Brazil. This compass is not a toy. It is a map."

Marina blushed. Leo was very cute. "Can you help me?" she asked.

"Yes," Leo said. "But we cannot stay here. It is dangerous. The compass is pointing North... to the Amazon Rainforest."

  • Shelves (Noun): Flat boards on a wall or cupboard where you put books.
  • To Drop (Verb): To let something fall by accident.
  • Handsome (Adjective): A word used to describe a man who looks good/attractive.
  • Shocked (Adjective): Feeling very surprised.
  • Legend (Noun): An old story from the past that might be true or false.
  • To Blush (Verb): When your face turns red because you are shy or embarrassed.

Chapter 3: The Green Journey

Setting: Leaving the Farmhouse / On the Road
Mood: Exciting, Suspicious, Adventurous

Marina ran back to the farmhouse. She packed her bags quickly. She took her favorite pink dress, a warm jacket, and the magical compass. Leo was waiting outside in his old, green Jeep.

Suddenly, Aunt Vanda stood at the door. She did not look happy.

"Where are you going, Marina?" Vanda asked. Her voice was cold.

"I... I am going on a trip with Leo," Marina said nervously. "We are going to visit the Amazon."

Vanda looked at Leo and then at the compass in Marina's hand. Her expression changed. She smiled, but the smile did not reach her eyes.

"The Amazon is dangerous," Vanda said. "You need protection. I will come with you."

Marina was surprised. She wanted to say no, but she was too polite. "Okay, Auntie. You can come."

Leo looked worried, but he started the car. The three of them drove away from the farmhouse. The sun was setting, and the road to the North was long. The adventure was beginning, but Marina felt a strange chill in the air.

  • Worried (Adjective): Thinking about bad things that might happen; anxious.
  • To Set (Verb): When the sun goes down at the end of the day (Sunset).
  • Chill (Noun): A sudden feeling of cold, often because of fear.

Chapter 4: The Emerald of the River

Setting: The Amazon Rainforest / Manaus
Mood: Magical, Wet, Wonder

After a long drive, they arrived in Manaus. The air was hot and humid. Marina looked at the giant river. It was brown and vast.

"We need a boat," Leo said. He rented a small blue canoe. Aunt Vanda complained about the heat, but she sat in the back of the boat.

They traveled to the "Meeting of Waters," where the black river meets the brown river. It was beautiful. Suddenly, the pink compass in Marina's hand glowed very bright. It pointed to a quiet lagoon full of giant water lilies (Vit√≥ria-R√©gia).

Chapter 5: Shadows in the Jungle

Setting: A Jungle Lodge (Nighttime)
Mood: Scary, Romantic, Tense

That night, they slept in a small wooden lodge in the forest. The sounds of the jungle were loud. There were monkeys and birds calling.

Marina could not sleep. She went outside to look at the stars. The tiara was safe in her bag, but she felt anxious.

Suddenly, she heard a branch break. Snap!

"Who is there?" she asked.

A dark shadow moved behind a tree. It was not an animal. It looked like a person. Marina was scared. She stepped back and almost fell, but strong arms caught her.

It was Leo.

"Are you okay?" he asked softly. He held her close.

"I saw someone," Marina whispered. "I think someone is following us."

Leo looked into the dark forest, but the shadow was gone. "I will protect you, Marina," he said. "I promise."

They stood very close under the moonlight. Marina looked at Leo's lips. She wanted to kiss him, but suddenly Aunt Vanda opened her door. "Go to sleep!" she shouted. The romantic moment was over.

Vocabulary

  • Lodge (Noun): A small house or hotel in the countryside or forest.
  • Branch (Noun): A part of a tree that grows out from the trunk.
  • Shadow (Noun): A dark shape made when something blocks the light.
  • To Catch (Verb): To hold something that is falling (Past: Caught).
  • Softly (Adverb): In a gentle or quiet way.
  • Moonlight (Noun): The light that comes from the moon.
  • To Shout (Verb): To speak very loudly (usually in anger).

Chapter 7: The Rhythm of Magic

Setting: Inside the Golden Church
Mood: Spiritual, Ancient, Awe-inspiring

Marina and Leo entered the famous church. It was incredible. The walls were covered in gold. It was quiet and cool inside.

The pink compass pulled Marina toward the main altar.

Marina reached behind the statue. Her hand touched something cold metal. She pulled out a long, golden rod with a pink crystal on top. It was the Golden Scepter.

"They are connecting," Leo said. "We have all the pieces now: The Map, The Tiara, and The Scepter."
