Prologue: The Silver Barrier

The Hills of São Paulo (1800s) • Ancient, Mythic, Dark

The world you see is not the only world. There is a world of shadows, hidden behind the rain and the fog.

Long ago, creatures from the "Dark Dimension" hunted humans in Brazil. They were hungry and cruel. But humanity had protectors: The Wardens. These warriors did not use guns. They used silver swords and magic symbols called Runes.

In 1890, a Master Warden arrived in São Paulo. He stood on the highest hill. He drew a powerful Rune on the ground with silver dust. This Rune created a Barrier. It was an invisible wall of light.

For more than 100 years, the Barrier kept the monsters asleep. The city grew. Skyscrapers replaced the forest. People forgot the magic.

But now, the silver light is fading. The Rune is breaking. The monsters are waking up, and they smell fresh blood.

Lexicon

Hidden (Adjective) Put in a place where it cannot be seen.
Cruel (Adjective) Causing pain to others on purpose; very mean.
Protector (Noun) Someone who keeps others safe.
Dust (Noun) Fine powder (here, magical silver powder).
Invisible (Adjective) Cannot be seen by the eyes.
Skyscraper (Noun) A very tall building in a city.
To Fade (Verb) To slowly disappear or lose brightness.

3. What is happening to the Barrier now?

Chapter 1: The Shadow Hound

Downtown São Paulo (A Rainy Night) • Suspenseful, Gritty, Neon

The rain in São Paulo was heavy. It washed the grey streets of the city center. Gabriel adjusted his camera lens. He was a photography student, and he loved the old architecture at night. The neon signs reflected on the wet pavement: blue, pink, and red.

He pointed his camera at the Martinelli Building. Through the lens, he saw a stone gargoyle on the roof. It was ugly and terrifying.

Suddenly, the gargoyle moved. Blink.

"I need to sleep," Gabriel whispered. "My eyes are playing tricks on me."

He started walking towards the subway. The streets were empty. But he felt a strange chill. He felt like someone was watching him. He walked into a dark alleyway to take a shortcut.

Grrrrr...

He turned around. Two yellow eyes glowed in the darkness. It was a dog, but it was huge. Its skin was made of black smoke. It was a Shadow Hound.

The monster jumped. Gabriel screamed and covered his face.

SWISH!

A bright silver light cut through the darkness. The monster howled and dissolved into grey ash. Standing in front of Gabriel was a girl. She had short purple hair and wore a long leather coat. She held a glowing silver dagger.

She turned to Gabriel. Her eyes were dark and intense.

"You have the Sight," she said calmly. "You shouldn't be out this late... unless you want to be dinner."

Lexicon

To Reflect (Verb) To throw back light (like a mirror or water).
Pavement (Noun) The hard surface of a street or sidewalk.
Lens (Noun) The glass part of a camera.
Trick (Noun) An illusion; something that is not real.
Alleyway (Noun) A narrow street between buildings.
To Glow (Verb) To produce a soft light in the dark.
Ash (Noun) The grey powder left after something burns.

Comprehension

2. What attacked Gabriel?
3. Who saved Gabriel?

Chapter 2: The Sanctuary

An Antique Shop in Liberdade • Cozy, Magical, Secretive

Gabriel couldn't breathe. "What... what was that?" he asked.

The girl didn't answer. She grabbed his arm. Her grip was strong. "Not here. It's not safe. Follow me."

She pulled him through the rain to the Liberdade district. They stopped in front of an old antique shop full of dusty clocks and lamps. The sign said: Curiosidades Orientais.

She touched a symbol on the glass door. It glowed blue. Click. The door opened.

Inside, it was warm. The air smelled like old paper and tea. But Gabriel saw strange things: swords on the wall, jars with glowing liquids, and books written in a language he didn't know.

"My name is Isabela," the girl said. She took off her leather coat. She had a silver tattoo on her neck. It looked like the symbol on the door.

"Isabela... that dog... it was made of smoke," Gabriel stammered.

"That was a Scout," Isabela said. She looked at Gabriel with curiosity. "Normal humans cannot see them. They just see a stray dog. But you... you saw the smoke. You saw the eyes."

She stepped closer to him. Gabriel felt a strange electricity.

"Who are you, Gabriel?" she whispered. "And why do you have the Sight?"

Antique (Adjective) Very old and valuable.
Dusty (Adjective) Covered in dust (not clean).
Jar (Noun) A glass container for food or liquids.
Liquid (Noun) Something that flows like water (not solid).
Stray (Adjective) Homeless; without a home (usually for animals).
To Stammer (Verb) To speak with difficulty because of fear or nervousness.

Chapter 3: The Refusal

Inside the Shop / The Training Room • Tense, Romantic tension, Denial

"I am just a student!" Gabriel shouted. "I study architecture. I take photos. I don't want to be part of your magic club!"

He turned to the door. He wanted to leave. This was too crazy.

"You can leave," Isabela said quietly. She was cleaning her silver dagger with a cloth. "But the monsters know your scent now. They know you can see them. They will hunt you."

Gabriel stopped. He remembered the yellow eyes. He shivered.

"What are you?" Gabriel asked.

"I am a Warden. We protect the city," she said. She looked tired. For a second, she didn't look like a warrior. She just looked like a lonely girl.

Gabriel looked at her—really looked at her. She was beautiful, but dangerous. Like a storm.

"If I stay..." Gabriel said, stepping closer. "Will you teach me?"

Isabela looked up. Her dark eyes met his. The air in the room felt heavy and hot.

"I can teach you to run," she said. "I can teach you to hide. But I don't know if you can fight. The Midnight Guard is not a game, Gabriel. People die."

"I don't want to die," Gabriel said. "But I don't want to be blind anymore."

To Hunt (Verb) To chase and try to catch/kill.
To Shiver (Verb) To shake slightly because you are cold or frightened.
Warrior (Noun) A skilled fighter.
Lonely (Adjective) Sad because you have no friends or company.
Blind (Adjective) Unable to see.

Chapter 4: The Stolen Key

The Underground Base / Map Room • Investigative, Urgent

The next evening, Gabriel returned to the shop. Isabela opened a secret door behind a bookshelf. Downstairs, there was a modern room with computers and maps of São Paulo glowing on the walls.

An older man was there. He had white hair and a scar on his face. This was Commander Silas.

"Is this the boy with the Sight?" Silas asked. His voice was rough.

"Yes, Commander," Isabela said. "His name is Gabriel."

Silas looked at a digital map. A red light was blinking on Avenida Paulista.

"We have a problem," Silas said. "Last night, someone broke into the MASP museum. They didn't steal a painting. They stole the Obsidian Key."

"The Obsidian Key?" Isabela gasped. She looked pale. "But that key opens the..."

"Yes," Silas interrupted. "It opens the Tomb of the First Monster. If they use it, the Barrier will break completely."

Isabela looked at Gabriel. "We have to go. Now."

"Me?" Gabriel asked.

"You know the city," Isabela said, handing him a flashlight. It was heavy and made of silver. "And you have eyes that see the truth. We need you."

Scar (Noun) A mark left on the skin after a cut heals.
Rough (Adjective) Not soft; harsh (used for voices or surfaces).
To Blink (Verb) To flash on and off (like a light).
To Break into (Verb) To enter a place illegally/by force.
Tomb (Noun) A place where a dead person (or monster) is buried.
Flashlight (Noun) A small electric light you carry in your hand.

Chapter 5: Ambush at Ibirapuera

Ibirapuera Park (Night) • Action, Fast-paced, Magic

They drove a black motorcycle to Ibirapuera Park. At night, the park was dark and silent. The trees looked like skeletons.

"The tracker says the Key is here," Isabela whispered. She pulled out her silver sword. The Runes on the blade glowed blue.

"Stay behind me," she told Gabriel.

They walked to the lake. Suddenly, the water started to bubble.

"Isabela!" Gabriel shouted. "Look!"

He pointed to the trees. He didn't see a person. He saw a distortion in the air—like heat on a road.

"It's a Trap!" Isabela yelled.

Three figures jumped from the trees. They were Goblins, small but fast, with green skin and sharp teeth. They attacked Isabela.

Clang! Her sword hit their knives. sparks flew.

One Goblin saw Gabriel. It laughed and ran towards him. Gabriel didn't have a sword. He only had his silver flashlight.

The Goblin jumped. Gabriel didn't think. He shined the flashlight directly into the Goblin's eyes.

ZAP!

The light from the silver flashlight burned the Goblin. It screamed and covered its eyes. Gabriel kicked it into the lake.

Isabela finished the other two monsters. She looked at Gabriel, breathing hard. She smiled. It was a real smile this time.

"Not bad, Architectural Student," she said. "Not bad at all."

Skeleton (Noun) The bone structure of a body (here, referring to leafless trees).
Blade (Noun) The sharp metal part of a sword or knife.
To Bubble (Verb) When balls of air rise to the surface of water.
Distortion (Noun) When something looks twisted or unclear.
Trap (Noun) A plan to trick or catch someone.
Sparks (Noun) Small fiery particles thrown off by a fire or metal hitting metal.
To Shine (Verb) To point a light at something (Past: Shined/Shone).

Chapter 6: The Secret History

A Safehouse / Cafe in Vila Madalena • Quiet, Explanatory, Building Connection

After the fight, they needed to rest. They went to a small, quiet café in Vila Madalena. It was late, and they were the only customers.

Gabriel drank hot coffee. His hands were shaking. "I fought a Goblin," he said, staring at the cup. "I actually fought a Goblin."

Isabela smiled gently. "You were brave. Most people freeze."

She opened an old leather book on the table. It had drawings of the city from 100 years ago.

"The Obsidian Key opens the Tomb," she explained. "The Tomb is not just a grave. It is a prison. Inside is the Shadow King. If he wakes up, the Barrier will fall. Every monster in the dark dimension will enter São Paulo."

Gabriel looked at her. "Why do you do this, Isabela? You could have a normal life."

Isabela looked out the window at the rain. "My father was a Warden. He died protecting this city. I promised him I would finish his work."

Gabriel reached across the table and touched her hand. Her skin was cold, but his touch warmed her.

"You don't have to do it alone," Gabriel said.

For a moment, the war didn't exist. There was only the rain, the coffee, and the electricity between them.

To Promise (Verb) To say you will definitely do something.
To Reach (Verb) To stretch your arm to touch something.
Electricity (Noun) Power/Energy (here, a romantic feeling).

Chapter 7: The Traitor

The Underground Base • Shocking, Betrayal

They returned to the base to tell Commander Silas about the Goblins. But when they entered the Map Room, something was wrong.

The computers were smashed. The maps were dark. Commander Silas was standing in the middle of the room, holding a silver gun. But he wasn't pointing it at the door. He was pointing it at Isabela.

"Silas?" Isabela asked. "What are you doing?"

"I am sorry, Isabela," Silas said. His voice was cold. "The Shadow King promised me power. He promised to heal my scars. I am tired of fighting. I want to be on the winning side."

Gabriel saw something in Silas's pocket. It was the Obsidian Key.

"You stole it!" Gabriel shouted. "You stole the Key from the museum!"

"You are smart, boy," Silas laughed. "But you are too late. The ritual starts tonight at midnight."

Silas fired the gun. BANG!

The bullet hit a gas pipe next to them. Smoke filled the room. Silas ran to the elevator and locked the doors. He left them trapped in the smoke.

Chapter 8: The Race to the Tomb

The Streets / A Stolen Motorcycle • Fast, Desperate

Gabriel coughed in the smoke. "Isabela! Are you okay?"

"I'm fine!" she yelled. She used her sword to break the ventilation cover. "We have to climb out!"

They climbed through the air vents and came out on the street. It was still raining.

"Where is the Tomb?" Gabriel asked. "Silas said the ritual is at midnight. We have 20 minutes!"

Isabela looked at the city lights. "The old legends say the Tomb is under the Sé Cathedral. It is the center of the city."

They saw a motorcycle parked nearby. Isabela didn't ask for permission. She jumped on it and started the engine.

"Get on!" she shouted.

Gabriel jumped on the back and held her waist tightly. They drove fast through the traffic. The wind and rain hit their faces.

"If Silas opens the Tomb," Isabela shouted over the wind, "The Barrier will break. We have to stop him, Gabriel. Even if we die."

Gabriel held her tighter. "We won't die tonight."

Chapter 9: The Shadow King

The Crypt under the Cathedral • Dark, Epic, Scary

They ran into the Cathedral. It was empty. Isabela found a hidden lever behind a statue. The floor opened, revealing stone stairs going down into the darkness.

Down in the crypt, the air was freezing. Green fire burned in torches on the walls.

In the center of the room, there was a large stone door covered in chains. Silas stood in front of it. He put the Obsidian Key into the lock.

"Silas, stop!" Isabela screamed.

Silas turned. His eyes were black. He wasn't Silas anymore. The magic had corrupted him.

"The King is awake!" Silas yelled. He turned the key.

CRACK! The stone door broke open.

A cloud of black smoke poured out. Two giant red eyes appeared in the smoke. The Shadow King stepped out. He was ten feet tall, wearing armor made of bones. He held a sword of black fire.

Silas smiled. "My King, I freed you!"

The Shadow King looked at Silas. "Thank you," the monster said. Then, he swung his sword. Silas fell to the ground, lifeless.

"Traitors are not useful," the King growled. He looked at Gabriel and Isabela. "Now... fresh meat."

Chapter 10: The New Warden

The Crypt / The Aftermath • Heroic, Romantic Ending

The Shadow King raised his black sword. Isabela ran forward. Her silver sword clashed with the black fire.

CLANG!

She was fast, but the King was too strong. He hit her, and she flew across the room. She hit the wall and didn't move.

"Isabela!" Gabriel screamed.

The King laughed and walked towards Gabriel. "You have the Sight, boy. Watch your friend die."

Gabriel looked at the Runes on the wall. He remembered what Isabela said: The Barrier is made of light.

Gabriel grabbed his camera. It had a powerful Flash. He modified the settings: Maximum brightness.

He ran towards the monster. "Look here!" he shouted.

The Shadow King turned. Gabriel jumped and pressed the button.

CLICK-FLASH!

An explosion of pure white light filled the dark crypt. It was brighter than the sun. The Shadow King screamed. The light burned his shadow form. He dissolved into nothing.

Gabriel ran to Isabela. She opened her eyes weakly.

"You..." she whispered. "You killed a King with a camera?"

Gabriel smiled and helped her stand up. "Technology has its own magic."

They walked out of the cathedral. The sun was rising over São Paulo. The rain had stopped.

Isabela took Gabriel's hand. "The city is safe. But there are still monsters out there. We need a new Warden."

Gabriel looked at the city he loved. Then he looked at Isabela.

"When do we start training?" he asked.

To Raise (Verb) To lift something up.

Maximum (Adjective) The highest amount possible.
Brightness (Noun) The intensity of light.
To Dissolve (Verb) To become liquid or disappear (like sugar in water).
To Rise (Verb) To go up (The sun rises).
